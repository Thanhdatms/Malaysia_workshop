Session 02: AI Introduction
What is Artificial Intelligence?
Simple Definition

AI is software that can
think, learn, and act —
just like a smart assistant.

It doesn't follow rigid rules — it learns from examples and improves over time, just like a new employee getting better at their job.

🧠 Understand Requirements

Reads and interprets natural language — emails, documents, instructions — and extracts meaning accurately.

📊 Learn Patterns

Identifies recurring patterns in data and improves predictions the more information it receives.

⚡ Automate Repetitive Work

Handles tasks like drafting emails, summarizing reports, or filling templates — at machine speed.

🎯 Help with Decisions

Analyzes options, surfaces insights, and recommends actions based on your specific context and goals.

Session 03: Prompt Engineering
The Standard Prompt Template
5 Components
R
Role
Tell AI who it should be. "Act as a professional email writer..."
C
Context
Provide background. "I work in a logistics company. The audience is senior management..."
T
Task
State what you need clearly. "Write a summary of this week's sales performance..."
C
Constraints
Set boundaries. "Keep it under 150 words. Formal tone. No technical jargon."
O
Output Format
Specify the shape. "Return as bullet points / table / email / numbered list."
Full Prompt Example
You are a professional business writer with experience in logistics reports.

Context: I am preparing a weekly update for senior management at a distribution company. The report covers Q2 performance.

Task: Summarize the key highlights from the following sales data and flag any areas of concern.

Constraints: Keep it under 200 words. Use formal English. Avoid technical jargon. Be direct and factual.

Output format: Use 3 sections — Wins, Concerns, Next Steps. Each section should have 2–3 bullet points.

💡 Remember: The more specific your prompt, the more useful the AI output. Garbage in = Garbage out.

Session 03: Prompt Engineering
Simple Prompt vs. Structured Prompt
Side-by-Side Comparison
❌ Without Template
Write me an email about the project delay.
AI Output:
"Dear Team,

I wanted to inform you about the project delay. There have been some issues that have caused us to fall behind schedule. We are working hard to resolve these issues and will update you soon.

Best regards"

⚠ Vague. Generic. No specific action. Could apply to any situation.

✅ With Prompt Template
Role: Professional project manager. Context: Software project delayed 2 weeks due to vendor issues. Audience: Client (VP-level). Task: Write a delay notification email. Constraints: Formal, under 120 words, solution-focused. Output: Business email format.
AI Output:
"Dear [Client Name],

I'm writing to notify you that Phase 2 of the project will be delayed by approximately two weeks due to an unforeseen vendor delivery issue. We have already engaged an alternative supplier and adjusted the internal timeline to minimize impact.
The revised go-live date is [Date]. A detailed recovery plan will be shared by Friday.

We appreciate your understanding and remain committed to delivering a quality outcome.
Best regards, [Name]"

✓ Specific. Professional. Actionable. Contextually accurate.

Session 03: Prompt Engineering
Why Each Component Matters
What happens if it's missing?
Component What It Does ❌ Without It ✅ With It
Role Sets the AI's expertise and tone Generic, neutral response with no personality or depth "As an HR specialist, I recommend..." – targeted expert advice
Context Gives AI the background it needs AI makes assumptions; output may be irrelevant to your situation AI tailors the response to your specific industry and audience
Task Tells AI exactly what to produce AI may summarize, explain, or discuss — but not do what you needed AI produces exactly the deliverable you need
Constraints Controls scope, length, and style Output may be too long, too casual, or include unwanted content Output is concise, professional, and ready to use immediately
Output Format Defines the structure of the result AI writes in paragraphs when you needed a table or bullet list Output arrives in exactly the format you can copy and use

Key Insight

Each component removes one layer of ambiguity. When AI is unclear about any element, it fills the gap with assumptions — and those assumptions are rarely what you needed.

Reference Guide
📋
Task #2 – Reply to a customer complaint emailTas: Drafting professional replies takes 20–30 minutes per email

① Identify the Task

Writing polite, professional responses to unhappy customers is time-consuming and emotionally draining. Teams often struggle with tone and completeness.

  • Input: The issue customers are complaining about.
  • Output: Professional explanatory email to the client

Can AI help? Yes — AI can draft a full professional reply in under 30 seconds.

② Write the Structured Prompt

You are a senior customer service representative.
A customer ordered a product 2 weeks ago and has not received it. They are frustrated and demanding a refund.
Write a professional reply email.
Tone: empathetic and solution-focused. Under 120 words. No promises beyond what's standard policy.
Format: Business email with subject line.

Group Discussion Guidelines

After each team presents, other groups should:

  • Ask questions about the proposed AI use cases and outputs
  • Suggest ways to improve the prompt further
  • Share similar use cases from your own department
  • Challenge: Is AI truly the right solution for this task?
  • Identify potential limitations or risks of using AI for that task

Tip: There are no wrong answers — honest discussion leads to the best learning outcomes.
