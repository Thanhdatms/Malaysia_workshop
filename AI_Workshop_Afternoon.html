Case Study: After-Sales Complaint Handling
📋
CASE SCENARIO · AFTERNOON SESSION
GreenBuild Supply Co. — Customer Complaint & After-Sales Resolution
A mid-sized B2B supplier with 180 staff across 3 departments receives 60–80 customer complaints per month — covering delivery delays, incorrect shipments, and invoice disputes. The current manual process takes 3–7 business days per case, causing customer churn and staff burnout. Sound familiar?
3–7
DAYS TO
RESOLVE
📭

No Unified Intake

Complaints arrive via email, phone, WhatsApp, and a web form — scattered across 4 channels with no central tracking. Staff manually check each one and re-type into a spreadsheet.

⏳

Slow Cross-Department Handoffs

Customer Service logs the case, then emails Operations, who forwards to Finance if billing is involved. Each email handoff adds 1–2 days of waiting, with no visibility on status.

❓

Inconsistent Resolution Quality

No standard template, no SLA tracking, no feedback loop. Resolution outcome depends entirely on who handles the ticket — same complaint can yield very different results.

💡

Responsible AI Use
The 5 Real Risks of Using AI at Work
Know Before You Deploy

🔓 1. Security — Data Leakage

Employees paste confidential client data, financial records, or internal contracts into public AI tools like ChatGPT — which may use that data for training.

⚠️ Example: Sales team uploads customer pricing list to public AI → competitor data exposure risk

⚖️ 2. Compliance — Regulatory Violations

AI-generated content may not meet legal, financial, or industry-specific compliance standards such as PDPA, GDPR, or internal approval policies.

⚠️ Example: HR uses AI to screen CVs — risk of biased or non-compliant decisions

🔄 3. Consistency — Unpredictable Outputs

The same prompt submitted twice may produce different results. AI lacks a fixed, repeatable process — outputs vary by phrasing, context, and timing.

⚠️ Example: Monthly reports generated by AI differ in structure each time — hard to compare

💰 4. Cost — Uncontrolled API Spending

Heavy or unmonitored use of AI APIs (GPT-4, Claude, etc.) can result in unexpected monthly bills of thousands of dollars without proper governance.

⚠️ Example: Development team runs 10,000 API calls/day without a cost cap — bill spikes

🧠 5. Hallucination — AI Confidently Provides Wrong Information

AI models can generate completely false facts, fake citations, non-existent laws, or incorrect calculations — delivered with full confidence and professional tone. This is one of the most dangerous risks.

⚠️ Example: Legal team uses AI to summarize contract terms — AI invents a clause that doesn't exist → signed incorrectly

Risk Management
How to Minimize AI Risks
3 Strategies
👤
Strategy 1: Human-in-the-Loop (HITL)

A mandatory human review step is placed between every AI output and every consequential action. No AI decision goes live without human sign-off.

Validate Inputs: Check what data you're giving AI before submitting
Verify Outputs: Fact-check AI results before using or sharing them
Approve Actions: No automated action executes without explicit human approval
🔒
Strategy 2: Data Governance Policy

Establish clear company-wide rules about what data can and cannot be shared with AI tools.

  • Classify data: Public / Internal / Confidential / Restricted
  • Approve only specific AI tools for work use (whitelist)
  • Use enterprise AI plans with no training on your data (e.g., ChatGPT Team, Claude for Work)

Example rule: "Never input customer names, IDs, or financial figures into any public AI tool without prior IT approval."

📋
Strategy 3: Regular Auditing & Monitoring

Treat AI like any business system — it needs scheduled reviews, usage tracking, and quality audits.

  • Monthly spot-check: sample 10–20 AI outputs for accuracy
  • Track API usage costs weekly to catch anomalies early
  • Collect user feedback on AI quality (thumbs up/down logs)

Example: Customer service manager reviews 15 AI-drafted replies every Friday to ensure tone and accuracy remain on-brand.

Summary · Best Practices
5 Golden Rules When Using AI at Work
Remember These Always
1

🔍 Verify Before You Trust — Always fact-check AI outputs

AI can sound confident while being completely wrong. Never send, present, or act on AI content without independent verification.

2

🔒 Protect Sensitive Data — Never input confidential information into public AI

Treat public AI tools like a stranger on the street. Would you hand them your client list or salary data? Only use approved, enterprise AI platforms for sensitive work.

3

👤 Keep Humans in Control — AI assists; humans decide

For any decision with real consequences — hiring, customer commitments, financial approvals — a human must be the final decision-maker. AI is your advisor, not your authority.

4

🌱 Start Small, Scale Smart

Pilot AI in one department or one task. Measure results for 4–6 weeks before expanding.

5

📚 Keep Learning — AI evolves every month

The AI landscape changes faster than any other technology. Set aside 30 minutes weekly to explore new tools and updates.
